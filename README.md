# Employee-attrition-analysis-dashboard
Power BI HR Analytics Project | Employee Attrition Analysis Dashboard featuring workforce intelligence, attrition KPIs, employee segmentation, turnover analysis, risk assessment, DAX measures, interactive reports, and actionable business insights.

---

## 📌 Project Overview

The Employee Attrition Analysis Dashboard is an interactive Power BI solution built to analyze workforce attrition patterns and identify the key factors contributing to employee turnover.

The dashboard helps HR teams and business leaders understand:

- Who is leaving the organization
- Why employees are leaving
- Which departments and job roles are most affected
- Which employee groups are at higher risk
- What actions can improve employee retention

The project converts raw HR data into meaningful insights through KPI tracking, demographic analysis, behavioral analysis, and workforce risk assessment.

---

## 🎯 Business Objective

The primary objective of this project is to help organizations reduce employee turnover by identifying attrition trends and uncovering the factors that influence employee exits.

Key business questions answered:

- What is the overall attrition rate?
- Which departments experience the highest turnover?
- Which employee groups are most likely to leave?
- What factors contribute most to attrition?
- Which employees require proactive retention efforts?

## 📈 Key Insights

### Workforce Insights

- Overall attrition rate is 16.12%.
- Employee turnover exceeds the recommended benchmark.

### Department Insights

- Research & Development records the highest employee exits.
- Sales department also experiences significant attrition.

### Role-Based Insights

- Sales Representatives have the highest attrition rate.
- Laboratory Technicians and HR roles show elevated turnover.

### Demographic Insights

- Employees aged 18–25 exhibit the highest attrition rate.
- Single employees leave more frequently than married employees.

### Behavioral Insights

- Overtime significantly increases attrition risk.
- Low job satisfaction is strongly linked to turnover.
- Frequent business travel contributes to employee exits.
